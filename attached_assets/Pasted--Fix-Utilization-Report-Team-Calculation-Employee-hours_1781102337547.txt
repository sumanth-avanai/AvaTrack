# Fix: Utilization Report – Team Calculation & Employee hoursPerWeek

Three related bugs to fix. All fixes are logic/validation changes only.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Preserve ALL existing data. No schema migrations, no data loss.

---

## Bug 1: Team Util% – denominator excludes employees with utilizationTarget = 0

### Current behavior (wrong)
The team-level Util% denominator uses the available hours of ALL employees,
including those with `utilizationTarget = 0`.

### Correct behavior
```
teamUtil% = sumLoggedHours / sumAvailableHours
```
Where:
- **sumAvailableHours (denominator)** = available hours of employees with
  `utilizationTarget > 0` only
- **sumLoggedHours (numerator)** = logged hours of ALL employees,
  including those with `utilizationTarget = 0` IF they have logged hours

Employees with `utilizationTarget = 0` AND `loggedHours = 0` are excluded
entirely (neither numerator nor denominator).

### Example (Last Month)

| Employee              | hoursPerWeek | utilizationTarget | Available h | Logged h | In denominator? | In numerator? |
|-----------------------|-------------|-------------------|------------|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| Rohan Dhanawade       | 40          | 80%               | 173.3h     | 144.0h  | ✅ yes          | ✅ yes        |
| Keshav Reddy          | 40          | 50%               | 173.3h     | 112.0h  | ✅ yes          | ✅ yes        |
| Midhun Jose           | 20          | 80%               | 86.7h      | 0h      | ✅ yes          | ❌ no (0h)   |
| Christian Voigt       | 40          | 0%                | 173.3h     | 0h      | ❌ no           | ❌ no (0h)   |
| Veekshith S. Gurram   | 40          | 0%                | 173.3h     | 0h      | ❌ no           | ❌ no (0h)   |
| Punit Kumar           | 0           | 0%                | 0h         | 40.0h   | ❌ no           | ✅ yes (has hours) |

Correct team Util% = (all logged including Punit) / (available of target>0 only)
= 681.5h / 1386.5h = **49.1%**

The TARGET KPI card should also reflect only the sum of available hours of
employees with `utilizationTarget > 0`: **1386.5h** (not 1399.6h).

## Bug 2: Individual Util% for employees with utilizationTarget = 0

Individual Util% stays as-is: `loggedHours / availableHours` (unchanged formula).

However, if an employee has `hoursPerWeek = 0` (see Bug 3), they have no
available hours → show `—` instead of a percentage in the Util% column.

For employees with `utilizationTarget = 0` but `hoursPerWeek > 0`
(e.g. Christian Voigt, Veekshith), show their individual Util% normally
(e.g. 0% if no hours logged). They just don't count toward the team denominator.

---

## Bug 3: Employee view – hoursPerWeek = 0 must be allowed

### Current behavior (wrong)
The hoursPerWeek input in the Employee view does not accept 0. External
freelancers (e.g. Punit Kumar) have no fixed weekly hours, so 0 is a valid
value. Currently workaround is to enter 1, which causes a wrong available
hours calculation (4.3h instead of 0h) and inflated Util%.

### Fix
- Allow `hoursPerWeek = 0` to be saved in the Employee form
- Validation: allow values from 0 to 60 (currently likely min=1, change to min=0)
- When `hoursPerWeek = 0`: available hours = 0, TARGET column shows `—`
- These employees are treated as external/no-capacity for planning purposes

---

## What does NOT change

- Individual Util% formula: `loggedHours / availableHours` — unchanged
- Allocation % formula — unchanged
- Color logic (green ≥ target, red < target) — unchanged
- All existing data — no migrations, no deletions
- The team Target % (goal %) calculation — still a weighted average of
  utilizationTarget across employees with target > 0, unchanged

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Team Util% denominator = sum of availableHours for employees with
      `utilizationTarget > 0` only.
- [ ] Team Util% numerator = sum of loggedHours for all employees (incl.
      `utilizationTarget = 0` employees who have logged hours).
- [ ] Employees with `utilizationTarget = 0` AND `loggedHours = 0` are
      excluded from both numerator and denominator.
- [ ] TARGET KPI card reflects only the available hours sum of employees
      with `utilizationTarget > 0`.
- [ ] `hoursPerWeek = 0` can be saved in the Employee form.
- [ ] Employees with `hoursPerWeek = 0` show `—` in the TARGET column and
      `—` in the Util% column (no divide-by-zero).
- [ ] Employees with `utilizationTarget = 0` and `hoursPerWeek > 0` still
      show their individual Util% (e.g. 0%) in the table row.
- [ ] Punit Kumar: after setting hoursPerWeek to 0, his row shows TARGET `—`,
      Util% `—`, but his 40h logged still contribute to the team numerator.
