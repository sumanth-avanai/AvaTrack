Task: Resource Planner — fix bar rendering, zoom levels, and filter behavior
(round 3)

IMPORTANT: Do not modify any Booking, Employee, Project, Holiday, or
Vacation data. This is a rendering/interaction fix only.

CONTEXT: This corrects issues from the previous two rounds:
- The "Xh free" indicator was rendered as its own separate block/column next
  to each day instead of inside the same bar.
- "Week" zoom showed a ~2 month range instead of a single week.
- There was no way to zoom via mouse scroll.
- Project tile filtering only allowed single selection, and did not reorder
  employee rows.

1. MERGED BAR WITH STEPPED PARTIAL DAY (replaces the per-day box rendering
   from the previous round)
- A booking segment (contiguous days with >0 booked hours for the same
  role/project) renders as ONE visually continuous bar spanning all its
  days — same color throughout, no gaps between days, only one role-name
  label for the whole segment, positioned at the top-left of the segment
  and never repeated per day.
- Each day within the segment has its own fill height proportional to
  booked hours / daily capacity for that day, anchored to the TOP of the
  row:
  - Full day (booked = capacity): fill height = 100% of the row height.
  - Partial day (booked < capacity): fill height = booked/capacity of the
    row height (e.g. 4 of 8h booked = 50% height), leaving empty space
    below within that day's column. In that empty space, directly below
    the shortened fill for that specific day, show the number of free
    hours as a small muted label (e.g. "4h"). This label only appears
    under the day(s) that are partially booked, not under fully booked
    days.
  - Overbooked day: fill renders in a warning color and slightly exceeds
    or caps at 100% height with a small "-Xh" label in red below/at the
    bar for that day.
- 0h days (no booking, or absence) are NOT part of the segment and break
  it, as already defined in prior rounds.
- Do not use the previous "role name left / Xh free right, same line"
  layout, and do not render "free" hours as a separate adjacent
  block/column — the free-hours indicator must sit within the same bar,
  under that day's own (shortened) fill.

2. FIX ZOOM LEVEL RANGES — add Week and Year, fix scoping
- Zoom options, in order: Week | Month | Quarter | Year.
- Week: shows exactly one calendar week (7 days).
- Month: shows exactly one calendar month.
- Quarter: shows the current calendar quarter (3 months).
- Year: shows the current calendar year (12 months).
- Prev/Next navigate by whole units of the selected zoom level (e.g. in
  Week zoom, Next moves forward exactly one week; in Month zoom, Next moves
  forward exactly one month, etc.).
- Fix the current bug where Week zoom renders a ~2 month wide range.

3. SCROLL-TO-ZOOM
- While the mouse cursor is over the timeline grid area (the area
  containing the date headers and employee rows), scrolling the mouse
  wheel changes the zoom level: scroll down/away = zoom out (Week → Month →
  Quarter → Year), scroll up/toward = zoom in (Year → Quarter → Month →
  Week). Clamp at both ends (no further zoom out past Year, no further zoom
  in past Week).
- This must work anywhere over the grid, not just over a dedicated control.
- Normal page scroll behavior outside the grid area is unaffected.

4. MULTI-SELECT PROJECT TILES + ROW REORDERING (extends the project shelf
   from the previous round)
- Project tiles support selecting MULTIPLE tiles simultaneously (toggle
  each tile independently — this is a multi-select, not a single-select
  radio filter). Clicking a selected tile again deselects just that tile.
- When one or more tiles are selected: employee rows reorder so that
  employees with at least one booking matching ANY selected project move
  to the top of the list (in their original relative order among
  themselves), and all other employees move below them (also preserving
  their original relative order), rendered dimmed as already defined in
  the previous round (booking segments dimmed, absence icons and
  "available" labels stay at normal opacity and are unaffected by
  dimming).
- When no tile is selected, rows render in their default order at normal
  opacity, as before.
- Reordering applies only to row position, not to which rows are shown —
  no employee row is ever hidden by this filter (highlight/reorder only,
  never hide, per the earlier decision).

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
- [ ] A booking with e.g. Mon-Thu at full capacity and Fri at half capacity
      (4 of 8h) renders as one continuous bar Mon-Fri, one role label at
      the top-left of the whole bar, with Friday's fill visibly shorter
      than Mon-Thu and "4h" shown below Friday's fill within that day's
      column only.
- [ ] Week zoom shows exactly 7 days; Month shows exactly one month;
      Quarter shows the current 3-month quarter; Year shows the current
      12 months.
- [ ] Prev/Next move by exactly one unit of the current zoom level.
- [ ] Scrolling the mouse wheel while hovering over the timeline grid
      changes the zoom level in both directions, clamped at Week and Year.
- [ ] Multiple project tiles can be selected at once; selection is additive
      (clicking one tile does not deselect others).
- [ ] With one or more tiles selected, matching employees move to the top
      of the row list, in original relative order; non-matching employees
      move below, dimmed (bars only — absence icons and "available" labels
      stay at full opacity).
- [ ] Deselecting all tiles restores default row order and full opacity
      everywhere.
- [ ] Existing Bookings/Employees/Projects/Holidays/Vacations remain
      unchanged in the database.
